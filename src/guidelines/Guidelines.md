# SAYA - Sustainable Agricultural Yield Alliance Guidelines

## Motto
"Growing Leaders. Growing Food. Growing the Future."

## Vision
To create a generation of empowered young agricultural leaders driving sustainable development, food security, and technological innovation across Zambia and beyond.

## Mission
To empower youth and farming communities through knowledge, sustainable agriculture, technology adoption, market access, entrepreneurship, and inclusive collaboration across all agricultural value chains.

## Core Values
- **Sustainability** – promoting environmental stewardship and long-term agricultural viability.
- **Innovation** – embracing modern practices and technology-driven solutions.
- **Integrity** – ensuring transparency and accountability in all operations.
- **Collaboration** – working with farmers, government, private sector, and institutions.
- **Youth Empowerment** – building skills, leadership, and opportunities for young people.
- **Food Safety** – promoting safe, toxin-free, high-quality agricultural products.

## Brand Promise
SAYA delivers empowerment, innovation, market access, and a strong voice for young farmers.

## PREAMBLE
We, the members of the Sustainable Agricultural Yield Alliance (SAYA), recognizing the importance of sustainable agriculture, livestock production, aquaculture, agribusiness, and youth empowerment, commit ourselves to creating a platform for knowledge sharing, capacity building, technological advancement, entrepreneurship, and stakeholder engagement.

We acknowledge the urgent need for food security, climate resilience, economic empowerment, and youth participation in agriculture. Through SAYA, we unite to transform the agricultural sector for the betterment of our communities and the nation.

## WHY SAYA?
Sustainable agriculture is essential in addressing global and local challenges such as climate change, food insecurity, unemployment, and poverty. Young people represent the energy, creativity, and innovation needed to transform the agricultural landscape.

SAYA exists to:
- Equip farmers with knowledge and modern skills.
- Create strong and reliable market networks.
- Build confidence in local farming communities.
- Advocate for policy reforms and youth inclusion.
- Introduce technology to accelerate agricultural growth.

SAYA is a movement, a platform, and a collective voice working toward a sustainable, innovative, and food-secure Zambia.

## ARTICLE I: PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

### Purpose
To enhance agricultural productivity, entrepreneurship, market access, technological innovation, and sustainable development through capacity building, strategic partnerships, and youth engagement.

### Objectives
- Promote sustainable farming, livestock, and aquaculture practices.
- Foster entrepreneurship, innovation, and agritech adoption.
- Provide platforms for knowledge sharing and networking.
- Offer skills development and professional training.
- Advocate for farmer-friendly policies and fair market practices.
- Support expansion of local and international trade.
- Promote food safety, quality control, and toxin-free food production.
- Strengthen bargaining power for farmers.
- Drive youth participation and leadership in agriculture.

## ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

### Membership Criteria
Open to individuals, organizations, students, cooperatives, and institutions aligned with agriculture, aquaculture, livestock, agritech, or sustainable development.

### Categories
- Individual Members
- Student Members
- Organizational Members
- Cooperative Members
- Honorary Members

### Benefits
- Access to training, workshops, and seminars.
- Agricultural technology tools and demonstrations.
- Market linkage support.
- Business development and mentorship.
- Networking with major industry players.
- Access to funding partners and financial institutions.

## ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURES
- **General Assembly:** Highest decision-making body.
- **Executive Committee:** Responsible for strategic leadership.
- **Sub-Committees:** Technology, Finance, Membership, Aquaculture, Livestock, Markets, etc.

## ARTICLE IV: ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### Chairperson
Leads SAYA, oversees operations, and represents the movement nationally and internationally.

### Vice-Chairperson
Assists the Chairperson and assumes duties in their absence.

### Secretary
Maintains documentation, communication, meeting minutes, and official records.

### Treasurer
Manages finances, reporting, budgeting, and financial transparency.

### Heads of Sub-Committees
Lead specific departments and initiatives (e.g., Technology, Extension Services, Market Affairs).

## ARTICLE V: MEETINGS & DECISION-MAKING
- General Assembly: Held annually.
- Executive Committee Meetings: Held quarterly or whenever necessary.
- Decisions made by majority vote or consensus.

## ARTICLE VI: ELECTIONS AND TERM LIMITS
- Leadership elections held every two years.
- Executive members serve a two-year renewable term, maximum two terms.

## ARTICLE VII: FINANCE
Revenue sources include:
- Membership fees
- Donations
- Grants
- Partnerships
- Income-generating activities (seedlings, trainings, produce sales)

Treasurer ensures transparent financial management with Executive Committee oversight.

## ARTICLE VIII: CONFLICT RESOLUTION
All disputes shall first be addressed through:
1. Mediation
2. Arbitration
3. Committee review

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS
Amendments require two-thirds approval of the General Assembly.

## ARTICLE X: DISSOLUTION
Upon dissolution, all assets are to be transferred to organizations with similar objectives.

## KEY STAKEHOLDERS AND THEIR ROLES

### Supermarkets, Hotels & Restaurants
Challenges such as inconsistent supply and quality standards limit local sourcing.
SAYA bridges this gap by offering trained farmers, consistent production, and food safety assurance.

**BENEFITS:**
- Reliable sourcing
- Strong local supplier relationships
- Improved brand image through sustainability

### Agro-Stores
Farmers often misuse inputs due to lack of knowledge. SAYA ensures informed, safe, and effective input use.

**BENEFITS:**
- Increased customer trust
- Promotion of safe farming techniques
- Market expansion

### Feed Suppliers
We encourage balanced feed practices and discourage harmful shortcuts.

**BENEFITS:**
- Farmer education
- Market visibility
- Healthier livestock outcomes

### Farmers
SAYA strengthens farmers through training, market linkages, and collective action.

**BENEFITS:**
- Higher income
- Reduced market exploitation
- Skills improvement
- Reliable markets

### Financial Institutions
They gain access to educated, lower-risk borrowers with improved agricultural practices.

**BENEFITS:**
- Reduced loan default risk
- Market expansion
- Strong community trust

### Government
By collaborating with SAYA, government receives accurate insights into farmer needs and promotes sustainability.

**BENEFITS:**
- Improved policy formulation
- Food safety and security
- Youth empowerment

### Local Government
Helps with markets, training centers, land access, and extension services.

**BENEFITS:**
- Reduced corruption through transparency
- Improved farmer–authority relations

### Churches
Important for community mobilization, moral guidance, and youth engagement.

### Mining Companies
They support community agriculture, food supply, and local development.

- Agritech workshops
- Demonstration farms
- Partnerships with tech companies

### Innovation Hubs
- Youth agritech competitions
- Incubation programs
- Prototyping and field testing

## CONCLUSION
SAYA is a youth-driven movement committed to transforming agriculture, empowering farmers, integrating modern technology, opening lucrative markets, fostering partnerships, and shaping future leaders. Together, we are building a sustainable agricultural ecosystem that benefits both current and future generations.
